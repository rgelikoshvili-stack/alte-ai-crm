# Official Academic Rules Structured Summary

This summary is prepared from the official academic rules and calendar evidence files in `docs/knowledge_evidence/official_academic_rules/`. It is a conservative chatbot source package only; production DB import still requires separate approval.

## Teaching Language

- The university teaching language is Georgian.
- Some educational programs are implemented in English.
- For program-specific language requirements, answer only from the specific program/regulation source and recommend Admissions/Academic Registry confirmation if the program is not identified.

## 2025-2026 Academic Calendar Dates

### Autumn Semester Midterm Exams

If the user does not specify a program, list the date range by program/category:

- Bachelor programs except Computer Science: 17-22 November.
- Computer Science GEO/ENG: 24-29 November.
- Master programs: 17-22 November.
- One-cycle programs: 1-6 December.
- First-year one-cycle English programs: 5-10 January.

### Spring Registration And Exam Notes

- Computer Science spring academic/administrative registration: 9-14 March.
- Additional/retake exam dates vary by program/category; answer with the exact calendar row if the program is known and hand over if the category is unclear.

## Credits And Program Volume

- Bachelor completion/diploma requirement: 240 ECTS.
- One-cycle Medicine: 360 ECTS.
- One-cycle Dentistry: 300 ECTS.
- Master program volume: not less than 120 ECTS where the regulation describes the master level/program volume.

## Student Status Suspension

Suspension grounds include written request, study at a foreign university, illness, pregnancy/childbirth, military service, tuition non-payment, registration/document issues, and other legal grounds listed in the regulation. Maximum total suspension period: 5 years. Personal status decisions should be handed over to Academic Registry/Student Services.

## GPA Formulas

- Course GPA formula: `GPA = (X - 50) * 0.06 + 1`.
- Summary GPA formula: `sum(course GPA * credits) / total credits`.
- FX and F count as `0` in GPA.

## FX/F Rules

- FX means 41-50 points: the student needs more work and has the right to take an additional exam once.
- F means failed; do not invent remediation beyond the regulation and hand over personal cases.

## Final Exam Admission And Additional Exam

- Final exam admission depends on regulation/syllabus prerequisites, including no financial debt and required minimum positive evaluations in mandatory/intermediate syllabus components.
- If the final exam was missed for a valid reason and prerequisites are satisfied, the student may take an additional exam.
- Additional exam is held after final exam results are announced, not earlier than 5 days.

## Bachelor Admission

- Bachelor admission is generally through Unified National Exams.
- Admission without national exams is possible for legally defined categories, including foreign citizens/stateless persons with foreign full general education, Georgian citizens who completed the last 2 years abroad, and other cases listed by law/ministry rules.
- English certificate requirements are mapped by level A2, B1, B2, C1; do not provide one universal level. Use the program-specific requirement where available.
- For administrative registration after national exams, the document list includes proof of payment of half of the annual tuition fee. For other categories/programs, answer cautiously and refer to the contract/admissions office.

## Master Admission

Required master admission documents include:

- ID card copy.
- CV/autobiography.
- One 3x4 color photo in printed and electronic form.
- Military registration certificate copy for male applicants.
- Notarized diploma copy.
- Diploma supplement copy.

Admission procedure includes the common master exam, university application/documents, internal university exam(s) in field and English and/or interview. Internal evaluation criteria include broad field knowledge, logical thinking, reasoned conclusions, speech culture, clear expression, and exact/comprehensive answers.

## Mobility And Credit Recognition

- Mobility credit recognition is based on comparing prior completed courses/program with the relevant Alte program.
- School and/or quality assurance evaluate compatibility.
- Student may need transcript, curriculum, syllabus, and additional documents.
- Never guarantee exact credit recognition in the chatbot; hand over transcript-level cases.

## Foreign Education Recognition

- University may require formal recognition, legalization and/or apostille.
- After university consent, applicant submits documents to NCEQE.
- After Ministry decision, university president issues enrollment order.

## Program Approval Process

- School/workgroup prepares the program.
- Quality Management and Compliance Department reviews and gives an expert conclusion.
- School Council discusses.
- Board of Directors makes the final approval/refusal decision.

- Teaching-learning excellence center function: needs additional official source; do not invent.
- Educational process management/student experience department: partially answerable only where the regulation supports operational involvement in organizing the learning process; otherwise hand over or ask for an additional official source.
